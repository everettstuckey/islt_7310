Key Insight

Students in lower-level math courses show significantly higher D/F rates, indicating a need for alternative pathways that build statistical thinking through applied, real-world data analysis rather than abstract algebraic manipulation.

Statistics courses with R programming provide a concrete, applied approach to mathematics that engages students through hands-on data exploration, particularly effective for students who struggle with traditional algebra sequences.

# Load patient data
library(tidyverse)
patients <- read_csv("clinical_trial.csv")

# Descriptive statistics
patients %>%
  group_by(treatment_group) %>%
  summarise(
    mean_bp = mean(blood_pressure),
    sd_bp   = sd(blood_pressure),
    n       = n()
  )

# Hypothesis test: does treatment lower BP?
t.test(blood_pressure ~ treatment_group,
       data = patients)

# Visualize results
ggplot(patients, aes(x = treatment_group,
                     y = blood_pressure,
                     fill = treatment_group)) +
  geom_boxplot() +

07 | Implementation Roadmap

Making It Happen

A phased approach to launching the HSSU statistics partnership at Collegiate School of Medicine & Bioscience.

Proposed Course Sequence

Junior Year, Fall Semester

STAT0260: Data Analysis & Statistics w/Lab (4 credits). Foundational statistics with R programming. Students learn data wrangling, visualization, and inference while building coding skills.

Junior Year, Spring Semester

MATH0301: Biostatistics (3 credits). Advanced statistical methods applied to biological sciences. ANOVA, experimental design, and non-parametric tests using R, SAS, and SPSS.

Senior Year

Apply skills to PLTW Capstone, AP Research, and summer internships. Students enter college with 7 credits and proven data science competency.

Logistics & Requirements

  • 👨‍🏫

    Instructor: Dr. Ann Podleski

    HSSU faculty member already teaching both courses. Can deliver instruction on-site at CSMB or via hybrid model.

  • 💻

    Technology: R & RStudio

    Free, open-source software. Runs on existing CSMB laptops. RStudio Cloud available as backup for any hardware issues.

  • 📅

    Schedule: 4 days/week

    STAT0260 meets Mon-Thu 1:00-1:50 PM. Can be adapted to CSMB bell schedule. MATH0301 meets Mon/Wed 2:00-3:15 PM.

  • 💵

    Cost: Dual Enrollment Rate

    HSSU dual enrollment tuition is significantly less than standard university tuition, making this accessible to all CSMB families.
