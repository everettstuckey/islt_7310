Four-Partner Ecosystem

Donald Danforth Plant Science Center

Global Leader: World-class plant science research institution in St. Louis

  • 400+ scientists, staff, students advancing plant science
  • Research in climate resilience, food security, bioenergy
  • $50M+ annual research funding from NSF, USDA, Gates Foundation
  • Educational partnerships with 50+ regional schools

CSMB - Collegiate School of Medicine & Bioscience

SLPS Specialized High School: Gateway to health/life sciences careers

  • ~300 students in biomedical/bioscience pathways
  • Hands-on labs, research projects, college partnerships
  • Dual credit opportunities with local universities
  • Direct pipeline to STEM careers and higher education

+ Metro East school districts served by JJK FAN: East St. Louis District 189 (~6,000 students), Brooklyn/Lovejoy District 188, Cahokia Heights District 187 covering Cahokia, Centreville, Alorton, Sauget, Washington Park, and adjacent communities

UAPB - University of Arkansas at Pine Bluff

1890 Land-Grant HBCU: Agriculture & aquaculture excellence

  • Aquaculture/Fisheries Center of Excellence - internationally recognized
  • Regulatory Science - USDA Center of Excellence, FDA/USDA pipelines
  • Agriculture programs - sustainable farming, agribusiness, food science
  • $15M+ facilities investment in STEM infrastructure

JJK FAN - Jackie Joyner-Kersee Foundation

Community Hub: Food, Agriculture, Nutrition Innovation Center

  • First-of-its-kind passive solar greenhouse in US
  • 30,000 lbs of food produced annually for $1/day operating cost
  • STEAM+Ag programming for East St. Louis youth
  • Entrepreneurship, nutrition, urban agriculture training

Career Pathways & Outcomes

Plant Science & Agribusiness careers are growing faster than average:

  • 9% growth projected for agricultural and food scientists through 2032 (BLS)
  • Median salary $74,940 for food scientists and technologists
  • $68,000+ median for agricultural specialists with bachelor's degree
  • Critical shortage of trained professionals in plant biotechnology
  • High demand for sustainable agriculture, climate resilience experts

Plant Science Scholarship

$5,000 Annual Award to UAPB

  • Eligibility: Students served by JJK FAN programs (East St. Louis District 189, Brooklyn/Lovejoy District 188, Cahokia Heights District 187, SLPS CSMB, and Metro East area students) pursuing plant science, agribusiness, or agricultural biotechnology at UAPB
  • Requirements: 3.5 GPA, demonstrated interest in agriculture/plant sciences, participation in JJK FAN or partner programs
  • Benefits: $5,000/year renewable for up to 4 years + guaranteed summer research at Danforth or JJK FAN
  • Priority consideration: Students completing internships at JJK FAN or Danforth during high school

Why This Partnership Works

  • Geographic advantage: St. Louis region has Danforth (world leader), JJK FAN (innovation hub), and strong connection to UAPB
  • Workforce pipeline: Addresses critical shortage of diverse talent in plant sciences
  • Authentic experiences: Students engage with real research, not simulations
  • HBCU pathway: UAPB provides culturally supportive environment with proven outcomes
  • Community impact: Food security, urban agriculture directly benefit East St. Louis and St. Louis
  • Sustained mentorship: 7-year relationship from 9th grade through college graduation
JJK FAN Innovation: The passive solar greenhouse produces 30,000 lbs of food annually using only $1/day in operating costs—a model for sustainable urban agriculture being studied nationally.
