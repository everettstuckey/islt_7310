Federal Grant Audit History

UAPB undergoes annual Single Audits in accordance with 2 CFR Part 200, Subpart F (formerly OMB Circular A-133). The institution has maintained a strong compliance record:

  • FY 2023 Single Audit: Unmodified (clean) opinion; zero material findings; zero questioned costs
  • FY 2022 Single Audit: Unmodified opinion; zero material findings; zero questioned costs
  • FY 2021 Single Audit: Unmodified opinion; one minor finding (documentation timing for travel reimbursements); corrected immediately with no recurrence
  • FY 2020 Single Audit: Unmodified opinion; zero findings

All Single Audit reports are publicly available in the Federal Audit Clearinghouse database.

Institutional Capacity for Grant Administration

Office of Sponsored Programs: UAPB maintains a dedicated Office of Sponsored Programs (OSP) staffed by a Director, two Grant Accountants, and a Pre-Award Coordinator. OSP provides:

  • Pre-award proposal development support (budget preparation, narrative review, compliance checks)
  • Post-award financial management (expenditure tracking, reporting, cash draws)
  • Subaward monitoring and management
  • Procurement and purchasing support consistent with 2 CFR 200.318-326
  • Training for Principal Investigators on federal compliance requirements

Financial Systems: UAPB uses Banner ERP system for financial accounting and Cayuse for grants management. These systems enable real-time budget monitoring, automated compliance checks, and efficient reporting.

Indirect Cost Rate: UAPB maintains a federally negotiated indirect cost rate agreement with the Department of Health and Human Services (cognizant agency). Current rates:

  • Instruction (Restricted): 8.0% of Modified Total Direct Costs
  • Organized Research: 48.0% of Modified Total Direct Costs
  • Other Sponsored Activities: 26.0% of Modified Total Direct Costs

Relevant Experience with Department of Education Grants

UAPB has successfully managed multiple grants from the U.S. Department of Education:

  • Title III Part B (Strengthening HBCUs): 5-year, $2.4M grant supporting infrastructure improvements, faculty development, and student success initiatives (2021-2026, currently active)
  • TRIO Student Support Services: 5-year, $1.3M grant providing academic support, tutoring, and advising for low-income and first-generation students (2020-2025, currently active)
  • Gaining Early Awareness and Readiness for Undergraduate Programs (GEAR UP): 7-year, $3.2M grant serving cohort of middle and high school students in rural Arkansas (2015-2022, completed successfully)

These grants demonstrate UAPB's capacity to comply with Department of Education reporting requirements, manage complex multi-year projects, and achieve strong student outcomes.
