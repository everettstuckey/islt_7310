Core Partnership: UAPB × Saint Louis Public Schools

UAPB and SLPS align missions to elevate college access, experiential learning, and bioscience career pathways for St. Louis scholars.

University of Arkansas at Pine Bluff

  • 1890 Land-Grant HBCU with top-ranked Aquaculture & Fisheries Center and expanding STEM, Nursing, and Agriculture facilities.
  • Dedicated Chancellor-level scholarship for SLPS seniors plus research, co-op, and alumni mentoring opportunities.

Saint Louis Public Schools

  • District-wide college and career academies preparing 1,200+ seniors annually for postsecondary STEM and bioscience majors.
  • Pipeline programs integrating FAFSA completion, dual credit, and internship readiness aligned with UAPB admissions milestones.

Regional ecosystem partners, including the Danforth Plant Science Center and the Jackie Joyner-Kersee Food, Agriculture & Nutrition Innovation Center ( JJK FAN ), expand bioscience education and workforce experiences that feed the UAPB × SLPS talent pipeline.

Flagship Scholarships Anchoring the Pipeline

Chancellor Scholarship (SLPS Reserved Seat)

  • $66,500 total value across four years for an SLPS senior.
  • Eligibility: 3.75 GPA, 25 ACT or equivalent SAT score, admitted to UAPB by January 1.
  • Launch with marquee announcement, district-wide application support, and alumni ambassadors.

Plant Science Pipeline Scholarship

  • $5,000 annually (renewable up to four years) for students from JJK FAN-served districts and SLPS pursuing plant science, agribusiness, or biotech.
  • Includes guaranteed summer research placements at Danforth or JJK FAN.
  • Priority for students who complete high school internships within the partnership.

Scholarships provide stacked financial support plus experiential learning to drive high matriculation and retention.

Activating the St. Louis Alumni Network

  • 200+ UAPB alumni reside in Greater St. Louis, forming a ready-made mentor, ambassador, and recruiter corps.
  • Establish a Golden Lions Alumni Ambassador program to lead school visits, FAFSA workshops, and application coaching.
  • Host monthly virtual touchpoints for admitted students pairing alumni stories with admissions guidance.
  • Showcase alumni success through media spotlights and celebratory signing events to amplify UAPB’s regional brand.

St. Louis Agribusiness & Bioscience Advantage

  • Danforth Plant Science Center anchors a globally respected research cluster with deep K-16 educational infrastructure.
  • BioSTL propels commercialization and industry connections, offering internships, venture support, and employer access for UAPB graduates.
  • JJK FAN’s Food, Agriculture, Nutrition Innovation Center delivers authentic urban agriculture training and entrepreneurial experiences.
  • Position UAPB as the HBCU gateway into the Midwest’s fastest-growing ag-tech corridor.

Recruitment Campaign Execution

District-Wide Push

  • Comprehensive visits to every SLPS high school with seniors.
  • On-site workshops covering admissions applications, essays, and FAFSA completion.
  • Leverage alumni ambassadors and current students for peer-to-peer influence.

Brand Activation

  • Coordinate press events and social media storytelling around scholarship recipients.
  • Integrate Danforth, JJK FAN, and BioSTL partners into experiential recruitment days.
  • Celebrate commitments with district-wide signing celebrations to build momentum.

Programs & Career Outcomes to Spotlight

Signature Academic Pathways

  • Aquaculture & Fisheries Center of Excellence with federal and industry pipelines.
  • Regulatory Science producing FDA, USDA, and EPA talent.
  • Emerging STEM strength in Nursing Nanoscience, Biochemistry, and Forensic Chemistry.

Career Trajectory

  • 9% projected growth in agricultural and food science careers through 2032; median salaries $68K-$75K.
  • High demand for plant biotechnology, sustainable agriculture, and climate resilience expertise.
  • 90+ employers recruit at UAPB career summits, including major private-sector and federal agencies.

Federal Funding Opportunity

UAPB Robotics Futures: AI & Robotics Mentorship Pipeline

A YouthBuild grant (U.S. Department of Labor) to address the "AI Divide" by mobilizing UAPB alumni as technical mentors for FIRST Robotics teams across St. Louis and Arkansas. Learn more: YouthBuild program.

The Model

  • Train 80 UAPB alumni in AI pedagogy through a specialized micro-credential.
  • Embed AI/Computer Vision modules into FIRST Robotics curriculum for 12 teams annually.
  • Host 4-week "UAPB AI & Robotics Bridge Institute" for high school participants.
  • Provide culturally responsive mentorship connecting students to successful Black engineers.

Impact & Scale

  • 400+ students served annually across SLPS and Arkansas districts.
  • Direct pipeline to UAPB CS/AI programs through summer institute.
  • Replicable HBCU-led K-12 STEM engagement model.
  • 48-month project period (Oct 2026 - Sep 2030).
