Digital Literacy Curriculum

Unit 1: Foundations of Digital Literacy

Learning Objectives:

  • Identify Core Digital Citizenship Concepts: Define and distinguish between digital footprints, online privacy, cybersecurity threats, and netiquette principles.
  • Analyze Digital Risks: Identify potential privacy violations, security threats, and inappropriate online behaviors in simulated digital environments.
  • Develop Responsible Technology Guidelines: Collaboratively create classroom technology norms for respectful communication and responsible device usage.
This introductory unit establishes the foundational concepts of digital citizenship, safety, and security, moving beyond abstract rules to practical application.
Key Activities:
Interactive eLearning Module
Students begin with a self-paced module created in a tool like Articulate Storyline. This module introduces key vocabulary (digital footprint, privacy, cybersecurity, netiquette, phishing) through a series of short, engaging videos and animated explainers. To ensure active processing, each concept is followed by a quick check-for-understanding, such as a multiple-choice question or a drag-and-drop activity. This pre-training approach, informed by Cognitive Load Theory, ensures students have the necessary schema before moving to more complex tasks.
Gamified Simulation: "Digital Footprint Detective"
In this scenario-based game, students analyze a fictional student's social media profile (a mock "FriendBook" page). They must identify specific posts that could compromise privacy (e.g., a photo showing a house number), instances of poor netiquette, and potential phishing scams in direct messages. Clicking on a problematic element reveals an explanation of the risk, providing immediate, contextualized feedback. The goal is to find all the "clues" and earn a "Digital Citizen" badge, making learning engaging and intrinsically motivating.
Collaborative Charter: "Classroom Tech Norms"
Using Microsoft Whiteboard within Teams, students work in small groups to brainstorm and draft a "Classroom Tech Norms" charter. They answer prompts like, "What does respectful online communication look like?" and "How can we responsibly use our devices in this class?" This co-creation process, rooted in Social Constructivism, fosters a sense of ownership and community, establishing a shared foundation for a respectful digital environment for the rest of the course. The teacher facilitates a final whole-class discussion to synthesize the group charters into a single class-wide agreement.
Learning Science: Grounded in Connectivism (learning as network navigation) and Cognitive Load Theory (pre-training vocabulary to reduce cognitive load).
EdTech Framework: Adheres to UDL principles (multiple means of representation, engagement, expression). The simulation represents SAMR's Modification level.
Standards Research: Targets ISTE Standard 1.2 (Digital Citizen). Gamification is supported by research for increasing motivation in digital literacy education.
