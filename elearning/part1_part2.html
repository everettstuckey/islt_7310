GOOGLEFINANCE Syntax

Syntax

=GOOGLEFINANCE(ticker, [attribute], [start_date], [num_days|end_date], [interval])

  • ticker – Abbreviation used to identify publicly traded securities. For example, "AAPL" for Apple Inc. You can also specify the exchange, e.g., "NASDAQ:AAPL".
  • attribute – Optional parameter specifying the type of information to fetch, such as "price" or "earnings per share". Defaults to "price" if left blank.
  • start_date – Starting date for fetching historical data. Optional; defaults to today's date.
  • num_days|end_date – Time frame for fetching stock information. Optional.
  • interval – Frequency of data retrieval: "DAILY" or "WEEKLY".

GOOGLEFINANCE Attributes

Attribute Description
"price" Stock price with up to 20 minutes delay.
"priceopen" Opening price at market open.
"high" Highest price of the current day.
"low" Lowest price of the current day.
"volume" Trading volume of the current day.
"marketcap" Market capitalization of the stock.
"tradetime" Time of the last trade of the stock.
"datadelay" Delay time for the real-time data.
"volumeavg" Average daily trading volume.
"pe" Price-to-earnings ratio.
"eps" Earnings per share.
"high52" Highest price in the last 52 weeks.
"low52" Lowest price in the last 52 weeks.
"change" Stock price change since the end of yesterday's trading.
"beta" Beta value.
"changepct" Percentage change in price since the end of yesterday's trading.
"closeyest" Yesterday's closing price.
"shares" Number of shares outstanding.
"currency" Currency the stock is priced in.
